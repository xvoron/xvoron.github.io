Lev Landau established so called theoretical minimum exams; two in math and nine in theoretical physics (one for each volume). This link has information about these exams (it is in English but all the links lead to Russian pages): Chair for "Problems in theoretical physics"

If you pass maths exams you should be able to handle most of the physics. I translated the requirements for each of the math exams for you from Russian in case you don't know it:

Math 1: You should be fluent computing indefinite integrals and solving ordinary differential equations. You should know Vector calculus and Tensor calculus.

Math 2: Complex analysis, should be able to use Cauchy's integral theorem to compute integrals, solution of equations using Methods of contour integration (Laplace transform), computation of asymptotic behavior of integrals, Special functions (Legendre polynomials , Bessel function, Elliptic function , Hypergeometric function, Gamma function ). The subjects in Math 1 curriculum were usually studied during the first and second year of the undergraduate program at the school from which the above institution recruits theoretical physics majors. The subjects in Math 2 were covered during second and third year if my memory serves me right. Stronger students were able to pass the math exams earlier.
